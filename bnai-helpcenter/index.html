Everyday Tasks
Front Desk
Answer the intercom & let a visitor in
Intercom · computer + phone · video
+
▶
Computer screen-recording added after install
  1. When a door intercom is pressed, the call rings on the Intercom Viewer and the reception PC, showing their live video.
  2. Click Answer to talk through two-way audio.
  3. Confirm who they are and who they're visiting.
  4. Click the unlock (door) icon to release the door.
  5. The door re-locks automatically.
▶
Phone screen-recording added after install
  1. Your phone gets a call notification from the UniFi Access app.
  2. Tap Answer to see their video and talk.
  3. Confirm them, then tap the unlock icon.
  4. The door re-locks on its own.
Tip: Answer from your phone when you step away from the desk — the call rings both places.
Front Desk
Screen a visitor before letting them in
Intercom · computer + phone
+
  1. The reception Intercom Viewer / PC shows live feeds from the public-entry doors.
  2. Click a door tile to enlarge its camera and see who's there.
  3. Use two-way audio to speak before granting entry.
  4. Release the door from the same screen once confirmed.
  1. Open the UniFi Access app and go to Doors / Live.
  2. Tap the public-entry door to view its camera.
  3. Speak via two-way audio, then tap unlock.
Front Desk
Find & export footage of an incident
Cameras · computer + phone · video
+
▶
Computer screen-recording added after install
  1. Open UniFi Protect in a browser and go to History (timeline).
  2. Pick the camera and set the date & time.
  3. Drag the timeline to the moment — motion events are marked.
  4. Click Export, set the clip's start and end, and download the file.
  5. Save it somewhere safe, or share with Atlas / authorities.
▶
Phone screen-recording added after install
  1. Open the UniFi Protect app and tap the camera.
  2. Tap History / Timeline and scrub to the time.
  3. Tap Export / Share and trim the clip.
  4. Save it to your phone or share the link.
Good to know: Your system keeps roughly 75 days of high-quality footage, with extra weeks at lower quality beyond that.

People & Access
Admin
Add a new staff member or volunteer
UniFi Identity · computer + phone · video
+
▶
Computer screen-recording added after install
  1. Open UniFi Identity in a browser and click Add User.
  2. Enter their name and email — they'll get an invite to set up the app.
  3. Assign them to a group (Staff, Clergy, Volunteers) — this controls which doors they can open.
  4. Once they accept, they can unlock with phone, Apple Wallet, fob, PIN, or face.
▶
Phone screen-recording added after install
  1. Open the UniFi Identity admin app and tap Users → Add.
  2. Enter their name and email to send the invite.
  3. Set their group to apply the right door access.
  4. They're provisioned the moment they accept.
Why groups matter: Set the door rules once per group, and every new person inherits them automatically.
Admin
Issue or revoke an NFC fob
Credentials · computer + phone
+
  1. In UniFi Identity (web), open the person's profile and choose Assign Credential → NFC.
  2. Tap one of your fobs to a reader to register it to them.
  3. To revoke a lost fob, open their profile and Remove the credential — instant.
  4. Need more fobs? They're a standard re-orderable pack — no vendor lock-in.
  1. In the UniFi Access / Identity app, open the person.
  2. Tap Add Credential → NFC, then tap the fob to a reader.
  3. To revoke, open the person and remove the fob credential.
Lost fob? Revoke first, then issue a new one. The old fob is dead the moment you remove it.
Admin
Set up face / Touch Pass unlock
Credentials · computer + phone
+
  1. In UniFi Access (web), confirm the door's Reader Pro has Face / Touch Pass enabled.
  2. Make sure the person is in a group that's allowed at that door.
  3. That's it on your end — the person enrolls their own face from their phone (next tab).
  1. The person opens the UniFi Identity app on their own phone.
  2. Under Credentials, they enroll their face — self-service, stays on their account.
  3. At a Reader Pro door, they're recognized hands-free and the door unlocks.
Privacy: Enrollment is opt-in and self-service — you don't collect or store anyone's photos by hand.
Admin
Set door schedules (e.g. school hours)
Access · computer + phone
+
  1. Open UniFi Access → Settings → Schedules.
  2. Create a schedule (e.g. "Pre-school 7:30am–6pm") and pick the days.
  3. Apply it to a door — stay unlocked, require a PIN, or lock per the schedule.
  4. Outside those hours the door defaults back to locked / credential-only.
  1. Open the UniFi Access app → Settings → Schedules.
  2. Add a schedule with times and days.
  3. Assign it to the door.
Heads-up: Building schedules is easier on the computer — the phone is best for quick edits.
Admin
Run an access activity report
Access · computer + phone
+
  1. Open UniFi Access → System Log (Activity).
  2. Each line reads in plain English: "Jane Cohen entered Door 3 with PIN" with the exact date & time.
  3. Filter on the left by Type (Unlocks, Access Denial), Location (door), People, or date range — e.g. "who entered the school wing Tuesday."
  4. Click ▶ on any line to watch the camera clip of that entry.
  5. Export as CSV for your records (attendance, incidents, office reports).
  1. Open the UniFi Access app → Activity / Logs.
  2. Filter and scroll the recent entries; tap one to see details and footage.
  3. For a full filtered export (CSV), use the computer.
School Access
Admin
Give a parent a PIN code
School Access · computer + phone
+
  1. Open UniFi Access → People. Open the parent — or Create New and put them in the School Parents group.
  2. On the person, open the ⚙ Settings tab → Credentials.
  3. Next to PIN, click ＋.
  4. Type a 4–8 digit code, or click Generate Random PIN.
  5. Click Add — it works immediately on the doors their group allows.
  1. Open the UniFi Access app → Users → tap the parent (or ＋ to add them).
  2. Credentials → PIN → enter or generate the code → Save.
Tip: Give each parent their own unique PIN — that's what makes the entry log show real names, not just "someone."
Admin
Remove or change a parent's PIN
School Access · computer + phone
+
  1. Open UniFi Access → People → the parent → ⚙ Settings → Credentials.
  2. Find their PIN and remove/delete it — access by PIN stops instantly.
  3. To change a PIN, remove the old one and add a new one.
  4. To cut all access (a family leaves), deactivate them — turn off their Access assignment or remove them from the School Parents group. Deactivate rather than delete, so their entry history stays on file.
  1. Users → tap the parent → Credentials → remove the PIN.
  2. Bigger changes (groups, full access) are easier on the computer.
Why it's better than a key: removal is instant — no re-keying a lock or collecting keys back.
Admin
Check when a parent entered (& how often)
School Access · computer + phone
+
  1. Open UniFi Access → People → the parent → the 📊 Activity tab.
  2. See a frequency chart (1D / 1W / 1M / 3M) — how often they come and at what times.
  3. Below it, every entry is listed with the door, time, and a camera snapshot of them at the door.
  1. Users → tap the parent → Activity.
  2. Scroll their recent entries; tap one to see the snapshot.
For everyone at once: use "Run an access activity report" to see all school-door entries and export them.
System & Safety
Emergency
Lock down all doors instantly
Access · computer + phone · know it cold
+
Important: Practice this once so it's muscle memory. Atlas walks your team through it during training.
  1. Open UniFi Access on the reception display.
  2. Tap the Lockdown button — every controlled door locks at once.
  3. Credentials are ignored during lockdown except designated emergency overrides.
  4. To end it, tap Release Lockdown — doors return to normal schedules.
  1. Open the UniFi Access app.
  2. Tap Lockdown at the top of the doors screen and confirm.
  3. All doors lock instantly — works from anywhere you have signal.
  4. Tap Release Lockdown to end it.
Set up by Atlas: We configure exactly which doors and overrides are included so the right people can still get out and first responders can get in.
IT
Check system health at a glance
System · computer + phone
+
  1. Open the UniFi dashboard in a browser — everything healthy shows green.
  2. An offline device turns red/grey and names itself (e.g. "South Lot camera").
  3. Note the device name if you spot something.
  1. Open the UniFi app and view the device list.
  2. Red/grey = offline; tap it for detail.
On your plan: With the Operations Partner tier, Atlas usually sees and addresses issues before you do.
IT
Safely reboot a single device
System · computer + phone
+
  1. Find the device in the UniFi dashboard and open it.
  2. Choose Restart — this reboots just that one camera or reader.
  3. Give it 1–2 minutes to come back green.
  4. Still down? Contact Atlas — don't pull power at the rack.
  1. Open the UniFi app and tap the device.
  2. Go to Settings → Restart.
  3. Wait for it to come back online.
Please don't: Reboot the NVR or switches yourself — call Atlas first so footage and doors stay protected.
Front Desk
Send a guest a temporary pass (QR / Wallet)
Access · computer + phone
+
  1. In UniFi Access, choose Visitor → New Visitor.
  2. Enter their name and the time window (e.g. just tonight's event).
  3. Pick which doors they may use and send a QR / Apple Wallet pass by text or email.
  4. The pass expires automatically — nothing to revoke.
  1. Open the UniFi Access app → Visitors → Add.
  2. Set the name, time window, and allowed doors.
  3. Share the pass link via text or email — it auto-expires.
